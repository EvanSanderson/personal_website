Week 1, General Assembly, 7.29.16

I'm currently half way through my first week of General Assembly's Web Development Immersive. So far, we've covered the basics of Git, Github, Atom, and CSS. I'm discovering the love/hate relationship with CSS which I'm sure all developers have - our instructor (Andy) showed us this GIF which I think sums up my general relationship with these aggravating style sheets:

In all honesty, I can say that I've learned more in the last 3 days than in the last 2 weeks, and that I know more now than I did a week ago. And that's a victory in my book. We also had a great panel with three professional developers. They talked about the industry, what it's like to be a developer, and left us with a great concept: if you don't feel somewhat over your head when you first start a job, you probably took the wrong job.
